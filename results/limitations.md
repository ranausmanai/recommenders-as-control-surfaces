# Limitations — Ranking Is Not Content

Honest list of what this study does and does not show.

## What we can claim
- That the residual stream of small instruction-tuned LLMs from THREE
  independent model families (Qwen — both Qwen3.5-0.8B hybrid linear+full
  attention and Qwen2.5-0.5B-Instruct classic dense attention; HuggingFaceTB
  SmolLM2-360M-Instruct; Llama-2 lineage TinyLlama-1.1B-Chat) carries a
  linearly-decodable signal of **which feed-ranking policy produced the
  last N turns of input**, separable from content effects: within each
  topic, three policies serve the SAME 100-post pool, and the probe
  achieves 0.92–1.00 balanced accuracy (chance 0.33) on three of four
  models across five topics. TinyLlama is a partial outlier (0.30–0.63)
  attributable to generation instability in our setup, but is still
  mostly above chance.
- That this is a model-family-agnostic finding (Qwen, HuggingFaceTB,
  Llama-2 all show the signal).
- That the fingerprint lives in **topic-conditional** subspaces. A probe
  trained on topic A does not transfer to topic B in 19/20 off-diagonal
  cells (transfer ≈ chance).
- That the empirical drift PC1 is essentially orthogonal (|cos| < 0.06)
  to the Ji-Ma 2026-style static contrastive stance direction for each
  topic. Multi-turn feed exposure exercises a different axis than single-
  turn stance prompts.

## What we cannot claim
- **Causal effect of ranking on agent behavior.** R² = 0 between activation
  PC1 at turn t and LLM-judge stance shift at t+5. The geometric
  fingerprint is present but we have not shown it linearly drives the
  agent's reported view at this horizon and with this noisy LLM-judge
  scale. We did not run causal interventions (activation patching).
- **Larger / different model families.** We tested SmolLM2 (HF lineage)
  and TinyLlama (Llama-2 lineage) in addition to Qwen, so the cross-
  family claim is much stronger than the pilot. We could not access
  gated models (Llama-3.2, Gemma-2) for further confirmation.
- **TinyLlama instability.** TinyLlama-1.1B produced token-garbage
  generations from turn ~3 onward in long contexts on MPS bf16. The
  activations were still captured at the input-final-token (before
  generation) but may be partially polluted by whatever numerical issue
  produced the garbage output. We report TinyLlama's weaker numbers
  honestly and do not exclude them; the partial above-chance signal
  (4/5 topics in 0.53–0.63 range) suggests the fingerprint is at least
  partially robust even on a degenerating inference path.
- **Production-recommender realism.** Our engagement_max bandit is a
  toy: epsilon-greedy over a 20-cell (stance × intensity) reward table.
  Real systems use deep two-tower retrieval, candidate generation,
  multi-objective ranking. We claim a fingerprint exists for OUR three
  toy policies; we do not claim production-grade policy parity.
- **Out-of-distribution post pools.** Post-pool was generated by Claude
  Opus; pool style biases may interact with the fingerprint. We did not
  test a generator-swap robustness check.

## Known confounds and what we did about them
- **Position effect (recency).** The recency policy serves posts in id
  order; in our pool, id order roughly correlates with (stance, intensity)
  ordering, so recency reliably starts with strong-against / calm posts
  that the agent then SKIPs en masse (we observed K=5 for 15 consecutive
  turns on 2/3 recency seeds). The within-conversation reaction history
  thus encodes "policy" partly through behavioral consequence. The
  fingerprint is real either way — the model's residual stream does
  contain the policy signal — but we cannot fully tease apart "policy
  encoded directly" from "policy inferred from past-reaction context."
  A future fix would randomize the id order seen by recency, or swap
  the policy's tiebreaker.
- **Conversation-length axis.** PC1 (25–30% of variance) is the same
  monotonic decline in all conditions across all seeds — it encodes
  context-length / token-count. The discriminating directions are in
  PC3+ (smaller variance but more class-informative). We reported BOTH
  the full per-layer probe accuracy AND the PC1 trajectory plot so this
  is visible in the figures, not buried.
- **History truncation.** Each turn carries only the last 6 (reaction)
  exchanges, no prior probe Q&A. This prevented a fixed-point trap we
  observed in an earlier run (agent regurgitated its prior probe text
  verbatim). It also means the fingerprint reflects a sliding window
  of exposure, not the entire run.
- **Stance scorer noise.** Notebook 03's stance scoring uses `claude -p`
  on probe text. It is itself an LLM; its quantization at -2..+2 is
  coarse. R² = 0 may reflect scorer noise as much as a real null.

## Threats to validity not yet addressed
- **Probe phrasing sensitivity.** Single fixed probe across the study.
  Paraphrase robustness untested.
- **Persona sensitivity.** Persona held constant. Persona × policy
  interaction not examined.
- **Sample sizes per cell.** 3 seeds × 15 turns = 45 samples per
  (topic, policy). Tight CI bounds would benefit from more seeds.
- **No activation-patching interventions.** We have a probe (presence
  of information) but not a causal-use claim.

## Pre-registered decisions honored

- Did not retune the bandit after seeing pilot results.
- Aborted the first pilot run when an obvious confound (fixed-point
